# Verath — World Lore & Design Bible

> **Game name:** Verath  
> **World name:** Verath (the basin)  
> **Status:** Locked — June 2026

---

## The World

Verath is a vast coastal lowland — warm, wet, impossibly fertile — ringed by the **Sapphire Reach**, a chain of inland seas and river deltas that keep the basin perpetually green. The ironstone veins here don't run through dead ash. They run through *living soil*, which is why the Rimwalker elves consider mining it a form of violence against the earth itself.

The basin is roughly oval, maybe three days' ride across at its widest. Its center is the **Verath Floor** — flat, fertile, laced with ironstone veins so dense the ground rings when you strike it. Its edges are the **Sapphire Reach** — a broken ring of inland seas and coastal cliffs that funnels every army toward the same contested ground.

There is no way into or out of Verath without crossing the Floor. That is why both factions want it. That is why neither can leave.

---

## The Creation Myth — The Ashening

In the beginning there was no basin. There was only the **Verdant Spine** — a mountain range so vast and so alive that its peaks exhaled mist year-round and its roots touched the molten core of the world. The Spine was not stone. It was *growing*.

The beings who lived in the Spine were the **Sylhen** — the First Tenders — neither fully physical nor fully spirit, tasked with the simple duty of keeping the Spine growing.

Then the **Ashfall** happened.

No one agrees on what caused it. The Iron Crown's monks say it was divine punishment for pride. The Horde's shamans say the Spine simply collapsed under its own ambition. The elves — who remember it directly — say only that *something came from below*, and that the Sylhen who tried to stop it were changed by the contact.

The Spine collapsed inward over three days. The ironwood burned. The roots, superheated by whatever rose from beneath, fused with the mineral ash and became **ironstone** — which is why the ore rings when you strike it. It remembers being alive.

The basin left behind was Verath. The Sylhen who survived were changed — slower to age, longer in memory, permanently marked by grief. The world knows them now as **the Rimwalkers**.

### The Secret
The Deepvein Find didn't just find ore. The Iron Crown's surveys disturbed something the Rimwalkers have been quietly containing for three centuries. The thing that caused the Ashfall is still down there. Still moving.

## Factions

### The Iron Crown (`aurex`)
**Tagline:** Steel · Banners · Order

The Iron Crown crossed the western Reach two generations ago, following an Auric Compact survey road abandoned for a century. What they found on the Floor was ironstone in quantities they had never seen — and Horde camps they considered squatters.

They are not cruel. They are inevitable.

**Goal:** Full control of the Verath Floor. Ironstone funds their kingdom beyond the Reach.

---

### The Raider Horde (`cinder`)
**Tagline:** Bone · Fire · Chaos

The Horde did not come to Verath. They *are* Verath. Gnomes have tunneled its eastern ridges for centuries. Gnolls have hunted the Sapphire Shores since before the Auric Compact existed. Trolls like Warren Maw remember when the Floor had no roads, no keeps, no surveyors.

They are not evil. They are cornered.

**Goal:** Drive the Iron Crown back over the western Reach. Not conquest — *reclamation*.

---

### The Rimwalkers (`rimwalker`) — Neutral / Future Faction
**Tagline:** Root · Memory · Patience

Descendants of the Sylhen. Living in the high ridges of the Sapphire Reach where ironwood still grows in scattered groves. Organized into **Holds** — small communities led by a **Tendkeeper** whose duty is maintaining the last living ironwood trees.

They know the Deepvein secret. They have been containing it for three centuries. Both factions are about to make their job impossible.

**Playstyle (future):** Defensive, terrain-control, vision-heavy. Buildings regenerate. Trains slowly. Wins by outlasting.

#### Key Rimwalker Figures

## The Conflict — The Verath Wars

Three years ago the Iron Crown discovered the **Deepvein** — the largest ironstone deposit ever found in Verath, directly beneath the Sapphire Shores. The Crown sent surveyors. The Horde sent them back in pieces.

The flashpoint: The surveys didn't just find ore — they disturbed something ancient. The Rimwalkers know what it is. Neither faction is listening.

## Heroes

### Iron Crown

#### Valdris the Ironwarden — Vanguard
*"The Crown does not ask if the war is just. It asks if the wall will hold."*

The general who built half the Citadel Keeps in Verath. Sixty years old, still fights in full plate. Sent to end the war in one campaign. Privately believes he is being sent to die doing it.

- **Passive — Ironwarden:** 25% damage reduction. Each hit stacks armor (max +40% reduction over 6 hits). Decays when he stops attacking.
- **Lv1 — Advance:** Charges 180px, knocks aside enemies for 90 damage.
- **Lv3 — Iron Edict:** Plants banner — friendly units within 240px survive one lethal hit for 10s.
- **Lv5 — The Last Wall:** Immovable + invulnerable for 4s, 25dmg/sec aura. Ends with 200dmg shockwave.

---

#### Seraphine — Channeler
*"The Ashfall took everything once. I will not watch it happen again."*

A young monk who channels something older than faith — a resonance with Verath itself. The Crown uses her as a field asset. She has her own reasons for being here.

- **Passive — Resonance Field:** Friendly units within 160px slow targets by 20% on every hit.
- **Lv1 — Attunement:** Target friendly unit gains +40% attack speed, +25% move speed for 7s.
- **Lv3 — Shatter:** 180px burst — silences enemies and deals 80 damage.
- **Lv5 — Verdant Surge:** Map-wide — all friendly units gain +20% damage and 4hp/sec regen for 8s.

---

### Raider Horde

#### Skrix — Saboteur
*"Every general has a plan. Mine is that yours falls apart first."*

Youngest war commander in Horde history. Carries devices nobody understands. The Iron Crown has a bounty on him. He considers this a performance metric.

- **Passive — Chaos Tax:** Enemy abilities used within 320px gain +2s cooldown.
- **Lv1 — Scatter Charge:** 4 bombs in a spread, 55dmg each — breaks enemy formations.
- **Lv3 — Unravel:** Target non-hero enemy switches allegiance for 4s.
- **Lv5 — Bedlam:** All enemies within 280px receive random movement commands for 6s.

---

#### Grollusk — Hex Shaman
*"You think the dead are gone. In Verath nothing is gone. Everything is just waiting."*

Ancient gnoll shaman. Speaks to every Horde member who died fighting the Iron Crown. Has a very large congregation.

- **Passive — Death Ward:** Gains 8hp each time a nearby friendly unit dies.
- **Lv1 — Hex:** Target enemy — 40% slower, 25% weaker attacks for 5s.
- **Lv3 — Death Coil:** 120 damage to enemy, 60hp heal to self.
- **Lv5 — Ancestor's Fury:** Summons 3 spirit warriors (80hp, 18dmg) for 12s.

### Rimwalkers (Future Expansion)

#### Aelindra Ashveil — Tendkeeper
*"I watched the Spine fall. I have been patient ever since."*

Oldest living Rimwalker. Was a young Sylhen during the Ashfall. Does not enter wars. Until now.

- **Passive — Ancient Root:** Cannot be knocked back. +4 tile vision radius.
- **Lv1 — Thornwall:** Roots block movement in a line for 8s.
- **Lv3 — Verdant Pulse:** 300px — heals friendlies 45hp, damages enemies 45.
- **Lv5 — The Ashfall:** 240px AoE, 3s cast, 300 damage + 5s silence. Named after the catastrophe.

## Ironstone

Not just ore. The fused remains of the Verdant Spine's living ironwood roots, superheated during the Ashfall. It rings when struck because it remembers being alive. Both factions mine it without knowing what they are destroying. The Rimwalkers know. They have always known.
